Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types,made in very wide, flat sheets. The same-named Italian dish is made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • onions
  • garlic
  • ground beef
  • tomatoes
  • tomato paste
  • ricotta chesse
  • lasagna noodle
  • basil
  • salt

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Sauce: Sauté chopped onions and garlic in olive oil, add ground beef until browned, then stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper; simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Cook the Noodles: Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  3. Mix the Cheese Filling: Combine ricotta cheese, egg, and chopped fresh basil in a bowl, then season with salt and pepper.
  4. Assemble the Lasagna: Spread a layer of meat sauce in a baking dish, layer with noodles, spread the ricotta mixture, sprinkle with mozzarella cheese, and repeat the layers, ending with mozzarella and grated Parmesan on top.
  5. Bake and Serve: Cover with foil and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes, then uncover and bake for another 15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden; let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
